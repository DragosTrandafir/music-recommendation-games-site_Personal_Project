Debut studio album

Born to die - a multifaceted album

Firstly, this album portraits the power of addiction, sexual obsession, fear and helplessness of one who relentessly tries to escape from these detrimental aspects of life. Even from the first listen, every track gave me a different vibe. The album simply captures your entire attention. Even though it is a pop album, I really think that even a non-pop fan would be absolutely astonished by this album.

Secondly, the most predominant feeling when listening to the album is nostalgia, it having proeminent references to 1950s and 1960s Americana. Furthermore, "Born to die" has an extremely cinematic and dramatic composition. Subsequently, the more you listen to the album, the stronger the feeling of nostalgia, melancholia and joy will take their toll on you.

It is also pivotal to mention that this is the most popular album from Lana del Rey, whereas you will never feel it is mainstream, because generally her music is original and has something that makes it special, no matter if you are a pop fan or not.
