Ultraviolence - a sad, psychedelic masterpiece

"Ultraviolence" is Lana Del Rey's third studio album, released by Polydor and Interscope Records on June 13, 2014. Originally, Del Rey had no plans to release another album after her major-label debut "Born to Die" (2012). The album also included contributions from other producers and has a more guitar based sound compared to Del Rey's previous work.

The album "Ultraviolence" by Lana Del Rey features a variety of musical styles, including psychedelic rock, dream pop, desert rock, blues rock, soft rock, and indie rock. The first track, "Cruel World", is a long, 70s-style rock breakup song. In the title track, "Ultraviolence", Del Rey references "He Hit Me (And It Felt Like a Kiss)" by the Crystals. "Shades of Cool" is a slow, gloomy ballad with reverberated guitars and Del Rey's vocals. "Brooklyn Baby" was written with Lou Reed in mind. "West Coast" combines elements of psychedelic rock, soft rock, and surf rock. "Sad Girl" is about being the other woman in an affair, while "Money Power Glory" is a sardonic reaction to fame. "F*cked My Way Up to the Top" is a commentary on industry perceptions and relationships.
